The Geography

Closer Than You Think

From most of North Fort Myers, the shop is a straight shot. Cross the river on US-41 and pick up Palm Beach Blvd (SR 80) heading east, or drop down I-75 and take the SR 80 exit. Either way you're looking at about 10 to 15 minutes — less time than most people spend in a chain-shop waiting room before anyone even looks at the car. The shop sits at Bayshore Ranch in Fort Myers, open Monday through Saturday, 7AM to 7PM, by appointment.

Why That Drive Is Worth It

Staying on your side of the river usually means a franchise counter: a service writer on commission, a laminated menu of add-ons, and a tech you'll never meet. Ten-some minutes east gets you the opposite — one certified mechanic who diagnoses the car himself, quotes the actual problem, and does the work with his own hands. When the fix matters more than the convenience, the bridge is a small price.

Or Skip the Drive Entirely

If getting the vehicle here is the hard part — it barely runs, you can't get away from work, or you're a one-car household — ask about concierge pickup and return. For an additional fee, Tony picks your vehicle up in North Fort Myers, brings it to the shop, fixes it right, and returns it to your door. Call (239) 271-4854 to arrange it when you book. It's the honest answer to "you're across the river from me."

The Work

What North Fort Myers Drivers Bring Over

Trucks That Actually Work

A lot of North Fort Myers runs on pickups — work trucks, tow rigs, gas and diesel. Trucks that haul in Florida heat chew through cooling systems, brakes, and front ends faster than the owner's manual admits. That's core work here, from diesel repair to brakes and transmission service.

Older, Higher-Mileage Vehicles

If your daily driver has six digits on the odometer, a dealership will happily quote you toward a trade-in. A good independent does the opposite: figure out what's actually wrong, tell you straight whether the repair makes sense, and keep a solid vehicle on the road for a fraction of a car payment. Diagnosis comes first, every time.

Florida Heat Casualties

This climate is brutal on machines. Summer traffic on US-41 will find every weak hose, tired water pump, and marginal battery you own. Overheating, A/C that quit in July, and electrical gremlins are everyday work — see cooling system repair and A/C repair for the details.
